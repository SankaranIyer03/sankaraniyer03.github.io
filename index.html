Portfolio

Undergraduate Data Science Researcher - Purdue Data Mine & UPS Partnership

Through the Purdue Data Mine, I had the opportunity to collaborate with UPS on a predictive modelling project. The project focussed on developing a predictive model that identifies high-potential companies for future partnerships based on previous partnership successes. Working as a group of graduate and undergraduate students, our objective was to accurately forecast the most promising opportunities for UPS. In addition to the model, we also developed an intuitive web application that visually showcases the model’s results. This allowed me to learn data science concepts such as supervised and unsupervised learning while also introducing me to modelling and testing.

Data Science Intern - GE Aerospace

As a Summer (2021) Intern at GE Aerospace, the largest aircraft engine manufacturer, I was part of the Aviation Test Analytics team. My project aimed to classify engine vibration patterns and identify anomalies for improved maintenance and safety in aviation. Using classification machine learning algorithms, vibration data was grouped to detect irregularities such as misalignment or wear. The model's accuracy was validated against real outcomes, enabling engineers to address critical issues effectively.

Supply Chain Intern - GE Aerospace

My internship at GE Aerospace was a pivotal experience that deepened my interest in digital transformation within manufacturing. By developing Python scripts through RegEx and Natural Language Processing (NLP) to analyze defective aircraft engine parts, I applied my domain knowledge in material properties such as strength, structure, and tolerances with data science skills. Visiting the shopfloor to validate my findings connected technical insights with real-world manufacturing processes such as chemical etching, shot peening, etc. This experience shaped my ability to bridge technology with practical applications, further fueling my commitment to advancing manufacturing innovation.

Project Manager, Teaching Assistant - Purdue Data Mine & John Deere

Post my stint as a student at The Data Mine, I got the opportunity to lead a year-long demand forecasting project for John Deere, leveraging statistical models like ARIMA and Exponential Smoothing to analyze complex time series data. As a Project Manager, I guided a diverse team of engineering, business, and data science students. This introduced me to challenges such as understanding various perspectives and it's value to the project, demonstrating the power of interdisciplinary collaboration.

ParkVue - Efficient Parking Solutions

My team and I had developed an innovative parking management system designed to optimize parking spaces in lots and garages through real-time availability tracking and smart reservation (ESP-32) technology. We participated in John Martinson Entreprenurial Center's (JMEC) Silicon Valley Boilermaker Innovation Group "SVBIG" competition, which helps fund startups originate from Purdue University. As part of the pitch, we won $10,000 as a kickstarter for the project.
